Data Checklist for Smarter Creatives

Before starting any design, a junior designer needs the answers to these questions. This is the key information required to create effective, data-informed banners and emails.

Pre-Design Briefing Questions:

  • What is the #1 Goal? (e.g., "Sell out the new 500-unit Lattafa perfume lot," "Drive clicks to the new Nike Footwear category," "Liquidate all remaining Samsung inventory.")
  • Who is the Primary Audience? (e.g., "Amazon FBA sellers," "Small retail store owners," "Exporters specializing in electronics.")
  • What are the Top 3 Selling Points for a RESELLER?
    • Examples: "75% below MSRP," "Top-searched brand this month," "High sell-through rate on Amazon," "New release with limited wholesale availability."
  • What Keywords are Customers Using?
    • Examples: "wholesale Apple lots," "liquidation pallets electronics," "bulk Nike shoes." This informs the headline and text.
  • What is the ONE specific Call to Action (CTA)? (e.g., "Shop Now," "Make an Offer," "View Lots," "Download Manifest.")

Practical Application: Banners & Emails

Single-Column Email Mandate (Mobile First)

All marketing emails MUST use a single-column layout. This ensures perfect readability on mobile devices, where a majority of our users open emails. It is non-negotiable.

Email Design Checklist

  • Single-column for perfect mobile rendering.
  • Critical financial data & brand name visible without scrolling.
  • Clear, benefit-focused subject line (e.g., "70% Off Apple MacBook Lots - 50 Units").
  • Large, obvious Call-to-Action (CTA) button in Primary Teal (#0D8082).
  • Follows all readability font size rules from the hierarchy.
